When you eat a scoop of ice cream, you can be sure that you’re getting milk and cream into your system. These ingredients are great sources of calcium, vitamin D, riboflavin, vitamin A, and phosphorus.


For instance, vanilla beans have antioxidants. Dark chocolate? It’s loaded with antioxidants and also has flavonoids to protect your heart, oleic acid to lower your cholesterol levels, and phytochemicals to fight off free radicals.


According to a study that was conducted at the Kyorin University in Tokyo, having ice cream for breakfast could be just what you need to give your brain a boost. The researcher compared a group of people who had ice cream with a group who had a glass of cold water first thing in the morningr.

Chocolate

Chocolate ice cream is a staple in home freezers everywhere and has long remained a favorite treat of dessert enthusiasts. You will find this frozen delight enjoyed by young and old.

Carbs - "Chocolate ice cream contains almost 19 grams of carbs, which come from high fructose corn syrup, corn syrup, cocoa, and guar gum, a natural thickening agent".
Vitamins and Minerals - Depending on the brand of chocolate ice cream you select, you can get up to 2.5 to 3 grams of protein per serving. The protein is found in milk and whey protein.

Strawberry

When it comes to indulging in a sweet treat, few things can beat the heavenly taste of strawberry ice cream. This classic frozen dessert is loved by people of all ages, with its vibrant pink color and refreshing flavor that instantly transports you to a sunny summer day.

Rich in vitamin C - Strawberry ice cream is a delicious treat that also provides a significant amount of vitamin C. This essential nutrient helps boost the immune system and promotes healthy skin.
Provides Energy - Ice cream is a great source of energy due to its carbohydrate content. The combination of sugar and dairy in strawberry ice cream provides a quick and tasty energy boost.
